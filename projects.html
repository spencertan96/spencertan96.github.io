Bullets and Beats

GAME
SCHOOL

A Rhythm/Bullet Hell Hybrid 2D game, inspired by the Touhou Project games with a Rhythm element added into the mix!

Players move with the mouse and collect ammo by interacting with "beats" at the appropriate timing.

There are enemies and bosses to defeat which fill the screen with enemy projectiles that the player will have to avoid (in classic Touhou fashion)! Players have to balance attacking and avoid the projectiles being shot at them.

This was a group project made for the NM3216 Game Design module in National University of Singapore. We used the Gamemaker Studio 2 engine, and the implementation was done by me and 2 of my groupmates. It took us approximately 2 months to implement the game, and I worked on it after the semester for about 1-2 months to add a few features.

My groupmates were in charge of the art assets, beats spawning and enemy behavior while I worked on the rest, namely player abilities and interactions, user interface, tutorials and miscellaneous stuff.

Treatment Database

APP

A C# Windows Presentation Foundation (WPF) application, purposed as a database for keeping track of treatments with additional analysis capabilities.

The basic features are adding treatment records with a Diagnosis, Symptoms and Treatments along with the Effectiveness of the treatments.

In addition to that, users can also search for specific records. It can also view the list of diagnoses, symptoms and treatments recorded in the database, with some statistics!

The app also has Analysis features. It looks at stored data and draws relations between them, displaying information such as the Most Common Diagnosis for a certain symptom, and the Most Effective Treatments for certain Diagnoses.

Some features were added to make record-adding more efficient, being able to add records easily through intentionally designed tab-based navigation.

Additional notes can also be added to records, diagnoses or symptoms for users to remember important information.

Random Path Visualizer

SCRIPT

A Python script to visualize paths, done as a personal project.

It requires 2 input files for visualisation, 1 being the image to use, while the other being the point graph that will be used to create the paths.

There are a few algorithms used to generate paths and it can be cycled through to see the different paths that each generates. Currently, there is the Greedy algorithm and the Dijkstra's Shortest Path algorithm.

Bezier curves can also be used to make the paths more natural looking! Without it, generated paths would just be a sequence of straight lines and look really unnatural.

Also, there is a feature to shift the paths such that they don't intersect with other members. An example is the image on the left/above!

Lastly, I added a feature to save the current generated path, so that future paths will always be a different permutation from the saved path.

Playing around in Unity

PROJECT

Having already learnt and messed around with Unreal Engine in university, I decided to try out the other big engine, Unity! I wanted to try and make a pretty environment and decided to attempt to create a waterfall and a lake.

I experimented with Unity Shader Graphs to make the shaders for the waterfall and the water surface. An example of the water surface shader is on the right/below! I scrolled 2 textures in different directions to get the ripple animation on the water, with vertex displacement to simulate waves!

For the waterfall, I used Blender to create the waterfall mesh, shown left/above! Then I used Unity's Particle System to simulate the water splashes at the bottom of the waterfall.

I had to make sure the UV mapping was regular so that when I scrolled the texture to simulate falling water, it would look realistic.

I was pretty satisfied with the results! There are definitely things to improve and add on but I learnt quite a bit about how to approach and implement the concepts.
